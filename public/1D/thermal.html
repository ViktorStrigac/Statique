Thermal load on beams and trusses

Let's denote the temperature of the top surface of the element as $t_{t}$, the bottom as $t_{b}$ and the temperature of the exterior as $t_{ext}$. The temperature along the height of the element is a linear function, and can be expressed as $$t(z)=\frac{{\Delta}t}{h}+\bar{t}-t_{ext}$$ where $${\Delta}t=t_{t}-t_{b}$$ $$\bar{t}=\frac{t_t+t_b}{2}$$ The strains along the axis of the element can be expressed as a function of coordinate $z$ as $\epsilon_x(z)={\alpha_t}t(z)$. The stresses caused by these strains will be $\sigma_x(z)=E\, \epsilon_x(z)=E\, {\alpha_t} t(z)$ The axial force is expressed in integral form as $$ \begin{eqnarray} N_x & \, = \, & \iint\limits_A{ \sigma_x(z) dA} \\ & \, = \, & \int\limits_{-\frac{h}{2}}^{\frac{h}{2}} \sigma_x(z) b \, dz \\ & \, = \, & \int\limits_{-\frac{h}{2}}^{\frac{h}{2}} E\, {\alpha_t} \left(\frac{\Delta t}{h}+\bar{t}-t_{ext}\right) b \, dz \end{eqnarray} $$ and after evaluation we substitute $t_{ef}=\Delta t - t_{ext}$, and we get the known formula for the axial force caused by change in temperature $$ N_x = E \, \alpha_t A \, t_{ef} $$ Bending moment can be caused by the difference of the temperature along the height of the element. It can be expressed in form of integral as $$ \begin{eqnarray} M_y & \, = \, & \iint\limits_A{\sigma_x(z) z \, dA} \\ & \, = \, & \int\limits_{-\frac{h}{2}}^{\frac{h}{2}} \sigma_x(z) z \, b \, dz \\ & \, = \, & \int\limits_{-\frac{h}{2}}^{\frac{h}{2}} E \, {\alpha_t} \left(\frac{\Delta t}{h}+\bar{t}-t_{ext}\right) z \, b \, dz \end{eqnarray} $$ which after evaluation gives formula $$ M_y = E \, \alpha_t b \, \frac{h^2}{12} \Delta t $$
